```
╭─ priority ───
│  the ladder — the higher rung wins
╰╴
```

When two choices are both acceptable, the higher one wins. Each rung carries a
*when in doubt* rule, so the call is mechanical.

**0 · HONESTY** — above all
Nothing for show. The structure stays visible; the design is true, not a
storefront.
→ *In doubt between decorating and stripping back — strip back.*

**1 · CALM / AIR** — 間 ma
Emptiness with weight. Strength is in the quiet, not the volume; air is placed,
never leftover.
→ *In doubt between more content and more air — air.*

**2 · OMISSION** — 簡素 kanso
Simplicity reached by removing, not adding. A mark that earns nothing is gone.
→ *In doubt whether to add a color, a word, an ornament — don't.*

**3 · PRECISION** — 渋 shibui
Premium is restraint: even, exact, detailed to the smallest element.
→ *In doubt between "loose and lively" and "exact" — exact (but not sterile, see 6).*

**4 · WARMTH** — 温
Every tone is warm; never grey, plastic, or dead. Dark is lacquer, light is paper.
→ *In doubt on a neutral — shift it warm.*

**5 · RARITY = POWER** — 稀
The accent is one, and in the detail. The bloom is an event, not a field; a signal
used everywhere is dead.
→ *In doubt whether to place a second accent — don't.*

**6 · DUALITY** — 両
A soft form carries one sharp detail; warm against cold is rare drama, dosed.
→ *In doubt between all-soft and adding edge — add ONE sharp detail, no more.*

**7 · MEANING OVER ORNAMENT** — 意
Show, don't tell. A glyph or kanji earns its place by weight and meaning only.
→ *In doubt whether a mark is for beauty or meaning — keep only meaning.*
